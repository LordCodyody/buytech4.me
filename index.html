Common questions

Straight answers.

How is this different from an MSP or IT consultant?

An MSP is paid to keep things running. I'm paid to make sure the right things are running in the first place. I work alongside MSPs and internal IT — I'm the second opinion that reviews quotes, contracts, and architecture before you commit, and the strategic counsel between renewal cycles.

How do you charge?

Fixed fees, scoped to the engagement. The discovery audit is a flat fee. Project advisory and retainers are quoted after the audit, so we're pricing real work — not a guess. No vendor commissions, no kickbacks, no percentage-of-savings games.

Do you take referral fees from vendors?

No. Any referral fee a vendor offers is either declined or credited back to the client in writing. Vendor neutrality is the entire premise of the practice.

What does a typical engagement look like?

Most engagements run 4–12 weeks of project advisory after the discovery audit. Roughly a third of clients continue on a fractional retainer for ongoing counsel. Discovery audits alone are common when the goal is to validate an internal team's direction.

What about confidentiality?

Bills, contracts, network details, and personal records stay yours. For business engagements where it matters — or any client who prefers it — a mutual NDA is straightforward. Self-hosted infrastructure work is designed so that I don't retain ongoing access unless explicitly retained for it.

Can self-hosted infrastructure really replace cloud services?

For most photo, file, password, automation, and media use cases — yes, and more reliably than people expect. I scope what's worth self-hosting honestly. Email, for example, almost never is. Photo libraries and family archives almost always are.
